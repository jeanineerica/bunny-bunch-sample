The Angel Program

The Angel Program helps sick or injured bunnies get back to good health.

Marlene

Marlene was spotted by a volunteer in a local shelter in Southern California. When the volunteer noticed Marlene was not in good health, she called Caroline to help. Marlene was then taken to Bunny Bunch in Orange County where Caroline noticed right away the discoloration of her fur and bald irritated skin, a direct result of being in a small, unclean cage. By first look, one would not be able to tell Marlene is bony and most likely undernourished. An emaciated body could be from a problem with the teeth, so it's recommended to check the mouth as well. It's important to remember to never bathe rabbits, they must only be groomed, however in some cases like this, Marlene will be bottom bathed to begin the healing process.

  • How to spot urine scald
    • Baldness and red, irritated skin
    • Fallen fur
    • Discolored fur

Marlene in the Angel Program

Marlene is settled in nicely and will be cared for by Caroline until she is back in good health. It is important when caring for any undernourished bunny, to not allow them to over eat. She will be watched closely and fed a balanced diet of hay, pellets, and veggies.

Bunny Bunch is able to care for Marlene through the Angel Program. All too often rabbits are taken to vets or shelters to be euthanized because their humans are not willing to care for them. It is very common for us to get a call from a person that has just found a sick or injured rabbit in the street. Our Angel Fund helps these rabbits.
